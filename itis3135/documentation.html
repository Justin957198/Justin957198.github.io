Introduction.

Overview

The Jump Start Espresso Machine is a high-precision espresso system. This documentation details the five core mechanical objects that ensure a perfect extraction every time.

To get started, ensure your machine is connected to a grounded power source and the water reservoir is filled with filtered water. Watch the temprture gage to ensure perfect brew.

Starting Checklist:

  • Ensure machine is plugged in and the power switch located on the back is on and green.
  • Open the liquid panel and fill the internal reservoir with filtered water - a list of other acceptable liquieds will be listed below.
  • The second panel located next to the liquid intake is where the coffee filter will be inserted along with the coffee powder of choice.
  • Choose an appropriate cup and ensure nozel is pointed down, Note: machine includes a trap to catch spilling coffee but doesnt have extirior drainage.

Heating element

The Jump Start Espresso ultlizes a two boiler setup, one lage tank holds water to be heated while a smaller tank and be used for milk. The heating will take time to reach baseline temprature 95°F and milk into foam. The tanks are headed from internal coils of wire that use passing electricity to generate raidiant heat. Note: In the even that the machine beings to outut metalic or smokey smells turn off machine emediently and use the included manuel to check parts.

Parts List:

  • Heating coil - copper incased nichrome.
  • Main and secondary tanks - brass.
  • PID controller.

Cleaning

For cleaning ensure the machine is turned off and unplugged. Then use hot water and allow it to flow through the liquid delivery system. Remove the folter and use a damp cloth to clean the internals. Coffee filters should aloways be replaced and shold be company branded for better coffee filtering.
